Day 1: Journey to Germany

~6-7 hours driving | 650 km

Drive from the Netherlands, breaking the journey with a strategic overnight stop in Freiburg, Germany. This makes the long first leg manageable and safe. Consider Super 8 by Wyndham Freiburg (~€58), Aparthotel Adagio Access Freiburg (~€70), or ibis budget Freiburg Sued (~€56) for accommodation.

Day 2: Into the Swiss Alps

~3-4 hours driving | 250 km

A shorter drive brings you to the heart of Switzerland. We recommend the Lucerne area for its blend of stunning scenery and family-friendly, walkable attractions. Consider Casa Goldrose (~$78), Luzern Youth Hostel, or Alplodge Interlaken for budget accommodation.

Day 3: Swiss Wonders & Italian Bound

~3-4 hours driving | 300 km

After a morning in Lucerne, drive towards Milan. A scenic drive-by of Lake Como is a must-do before arriving in the city for your son's requested destination. For accommodation in Milan, consider hostels with private rooms like Ostello Bello Milano Duomo, YellowSquare Milan, or MEININGER Milano Garibaldi/Lambrate.

Logistics & Preparation

A smooth trip is a well-prepared trip. Here you'll find essential information on driving regulations, EV charging, and a comprehensive packing list. Click each section to expand the details.

  • Germany: Motorways are generally toll-free for cars. No special sticker needed. (ADAC)
  • Switzerland: A mandatory annual vignette is required for motorway use. Cost is ~€45 (40 CHF). It's highly recommended to buy this at the border for efficiency. Driving without it risks a large fine. (Swiss Federal Customs Administration)
  • Italy: Most motorways are tolled based on distance. You take a ticket on entry and pay at a booth on exit. Costs are roughly €0.07/km. (Autostrade per l'Italia)

The Tesla Supercharger network is extensive. Plan charging stops to coincide with meals or play breaks. Avoid idle fees by moving the car once charged. Average Supercharger costs vary:

  • Germany: €0.25 - €0.58 / kWh (Tesla Supercharger Network, Statista)
  • Switzerland: €0.25 - €0.35 / kWh (Tesla Supercharger Network, Statista)
  • Italy: €0.30 - €0.80 / kWh (Tesla Supercharger Network, Statista)

Tesla also implements idle fees if a vehicle remains connected to a Supercharger after charging is complete, particularly when the station is at 100% occupancy. This fee is €0.50 per minute, increasing to €1.00 per minute if the station is fully occupied. (Tesla Support) Prioritize hotels with destination chargers to start each day with a full battery.
